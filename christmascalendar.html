Luke 2:7

The nativity scene, as we commonly know it today with Mary, Joseph, and the baby Jesus in a stable, was popularized by St. Francis of Assisi in the 13th century. Prior to that, depictions of the nativity varied greatly.

Matthew 2:2

"For we saw his star when it rose and have come to worship him."

__________

The Star of Bethlehem, which guided the wise men to Jesus, remains a mystery. Various theories suggest it could have been a supernova, a comet, or a unique conjunction of planets.

Day 4

Luke 2:8

"And in the same region there were shepherds out in the field, keeping watch over their flock by night."

__________

The shepherds mentioned in the nativity story were likely not highly regarded in society, but they were chosen to be among the first to witness the birth of Jesus. This choice highlights Jesus' mission to bring salvation to all people, regardless of social status.

The manger in which Jesus was laid is traditionally believed to be a feeding trough for animals. This humble setting symbolizes Jesus' identification with the poor and marginalized.

Matthew 1:21

"She will bear a son, and you shall call his name Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins."

__________

The name "Jesus" was not uncommon during that time. It derived from the Hebrew name "Yeshua," which means "salvation" or "the Lord is salvation." The name reflects the purpose of Jesus' mission on Earth.

Matthew 2:11

"And going into the house, they saw the child with Mary his mother, and they fell down and worshiped him. Then, opening their treasures, they offered him gifts, gold and frankincense and myrrh."

The visit of the wise men bearing gifts of gold, frankincense, and myrrh holds symbolic significance. Gold symbolizes Jesus' kingship, frankincense represents his deity, and myrrh foreshadows his sacrificial death.
